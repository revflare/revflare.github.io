Dual AI-Powered Sales Methodologies

Our system uses proven sales frameworks to qualify leads and create urgency through structured SMS conversations

SPIN Selling Methodology

The SPIN framework helps uncover customer problems and creates urgency by exploring implications before positioning your solution.

Ideal for complex services with clear problem-solution scenarios

Builds value through understanding customer pain points

Natural progression from problem to solution

S

Situation Questions

Gather essential background information to understand the lead's context.

Example: "What type of HVAC system do you currently have?"
P

Problem Questions

Uncover issues and pain points the lead is experiencing.

Example: "Are you experiencing uneven temperatures in different rooms?"
I

Implication Questions

Explore the consequences of not addressing the identified problems.

Example: "How much are these issues adding to your energy bills?"
N

Need-Payoff Questions

Help the lead articulate the value and benefits of solving their problem.

Example: "How would consistent temperatures improve your comfort?"

Sandler Selling System

The Sandler method establishes rapport, qualifies budget and decision-making, and addresses pain points before presenting solutions.

Strong qualification focus saves time with serious buyers only

Builds trust through upfront contract and transparent process

Positions your team as consultative problem-solvers

B

Bonding & Rapport

Establish a connection and build trust with the prospect.

Example: "I see you're in [Location]. How long have you lived there?"
U

Up-Front Contract

Set clear expectations about the conversation and next steps.

Example: "I'd like to ask about your situation to see if we're a good fit. Is that okay?"
P

Pain Discovery

Uncover the emotional and practical pain points motivating the purchase.

Example: "What problems is your current roof causing that led you to contact us?"
Q

Budget & Decision

Qualify the prospect's budget and decision-making process.

Example: "Have you set aside a budget for this project? Who else is involved in this decision?"
